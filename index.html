Francesco Libbi

Francesc is a postdoctoral researcher in quantum physics at Harvard University. He got his PhD at EPFL, where he focused on computational quantum physics. Francesco has worked on training large-scale robotics Foundation models, developing robots that write their own code, and inventing handheld data collection for robots.


Frances is a Staff Research Scientist at Google DeepMind , where he leads a small team working on self-improving Foundation models in robotics. He received his Bachelors in Computer Science and Mathematics at UC Berkeley, and his PhD in Computer Science at Princeton. He is interested in building algorithms that enable machines to intelligently interact with the world and improve themselves over time. Andy received Best Paper Awards from HRI '24, CoRL '23, ICRA '23, T-RO '20, RSS'19, and has been finalist for paper awards at RSS '23, CoRL '20 - '22, ICRA '20, RSS '19, IROS '18. He led machine learning as part of Team MIT-Princeton, winning 1st place (stow task) at the worldwide Amazon Picking Challenge '17. Andy is a recipient of the Princeton SEAS Award for Excellence, Japan Foundation Paper Award, NVIDIA Fellowship, and Gordon Y.S. Wu Fellowship in Engineering and Wu Prize. His work has been featured in the press, including the New York Times, BBC, and Wired.
